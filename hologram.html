THE HOLOGRAM

superposition in 3 dimensions · distributed storage, live

Superposition is holographic storage — distributed, overlapping, over-capacity. Here the hidden space is 3 dimensions (the holographic volume), and the model must pack many features into it. Each feature is a vector on the plate; watch them pack past 3 dimensions into a rotating polytope on the sphere — every axis carrying fragments of many features at once, exactly like every patch of holographic film holds the whole scene.

What you're watching. A toy autoencoder (out = ReLU((x·W)·Wᵀ+b)) with 3 hidden dimensions, trained live (Adam, constant importance, sparse inputs). The arrows are the feature weight-vectors in that 3D space, rendered as a rotating hologram-plate. At low sparsity it keeps only ~3 (one per axis); as sparsity rises it packs far more than 3 into the volume, arranging them as a polytope on the unit sphere and tolerating the overlap — that over-capacity is superposition, and it is literally distributed holographic storage (cf. Plate's Holographic Reduced Representations, ~1995). A from-scratch JS reimplementation built on Anthropic's Toy Models of Superposition (Elhage…Olah 2022, MIT) — math re-derived, trained live; the phenomenon is theirs.
